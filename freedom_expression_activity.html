Part A — Learn from the errors Foundation

These are the most common mistakes from the class translation. Study each one carefully before doing the exercises.

Chunk 1
The debate on freedom of expression
❌ Common error
Le débat de la liberté d'expression
✓ Correct
Le débat sur la liberté d'expression
Use sur (not de) when a debate is about a topic. De implies possession, not subject matter. Also: liberté de la parole is non-standard — the correct term is always liberté d'expression.
Chunk 2
has been revived in France
❌ Common error
a été réanimé / a été renouvelé
✓ Correct
a été relancé / s'est ravivé / a ressurgi
Réanimer = to resuscitate (medical). Renouveler = to renew/update. Neither captures re-igniting a debate. Use relancer, raviver, ressurgir for debates/controversies coming back to life.
Chunk 6
who had drawn caricatures
❌ Common error
qui dessinaient des caricatures (present tense / wrong tense)
✓ Correct
qui avaient dessiné des caricatures (pluperfect)
The drawing happened before the killing — so the pluperfect (plus-que-parfait) is required: avaient dessiné. Also: dessins animés = animated cartoons (TV/film), NOT press cartoons. Use caricatures or dessins satiriques.
Chunk 7
many have asked
❌ Common error
beaucoup ont demandé
✓ Correct
beaucoup se sont demandé
Demander = to ask someone something. Se demander = to wonder / ask oneself. "Many have asked whether…" = internal wondering, so the reflexive se demander is required.
Chunk 9
can still accept without difficulty
❌ Common error
peut encore accepter facilement
✓ Correct
peut encore accepter sans difficulté
Sans difficulté = without difficulty (exact translation). Facilement = easily — a different meaning that loses the nuance of the original. In translation, match the structure of the source text where possible.
Chunk 17
it is a fundamental democratic right
❌ Common error
il est un droit démocratique fondamental / c'est un fondamental droit*
✓ Correct
c'est un droit démocratique fondamental
Before an indefinite article + noun: c'est is always required, never il est. Adjective order: in French, most adjectives follow the noun — un droit démocratique fondamental, not fondamental droit.
Chunk 19
the press remain completely free
❌ Common error
la presse reste complète libre / la presse reste gratuite
✓ Correct
la presse reste entièrement libre
Entièrement / totalement = completely (adverb modifying an adjective). Complet(e) is an adjective, not an adverb. Also: gratuit = free of charge — completely different meaning from libre = free (unrestricted).

Part D — Nuance & Grammar Higher

D1 — demander vs se demander

Translate these two sentences into French, paying careful attention to the difference between asking someone something and wondering internally.

a) "Many people have asked the government whether the law is enough."

b) "Since then, many have wondered whether satire still has a place."

a) Beaucoup de personnes ont demandé au gouvernement si la loi était suffisante.
b) Depuis lors, beaucoup se sont demandé si la satire avait encore sa place.
Note: demander au gouvernement si… = asking someone. Se demander si… = to wonder.

D2 — Tense accuracy: pluperfect

Translate the following, ensuring the correct sequence of tenses.

"The murderers attacked cartoonists who had published caricatures of the prophet."

Les meurtriers ont attaqué des dessinateurs qui avaient publié des caricatures du prophète.
Note: the publishing happened before the attack — pluperfect (avaient publié) is required. The attack itself = passé composé (ont attaqué).

D3 — c'est vs il est — advanced use

Translate these three sentences, each requiring a different structure.

a) "It is a fundamental right."    b) "It is essential that the press be free."    c) "It is difficult to define the limits of satire."

a) C'est un droit fondamental. (c'est + indefinite article + noun)
b) Il est essentiel que la presse soit libre. (il est + adjective + que + subjonctif)
c) Il est difficile de définir les limites de la satire. (il est + adjective + de + infinitive)
Rule: c'est before nouns; il est before adjectives followed by que/de.

D4 — Register & vocabulary choice

The word troubler was used by some students for "deeply upset." Explain in English why this is too weak, and give three stronger alternatives in French with a brief English gloss for each.

Why troubler is too weak: Troubler means to unsettle, disturb or confuse — it is psychological and soft. It does not carry the emotional weight of "deeply upset."

Stronger alternatives:
• bouleverser — to deeply upset, to shatter emotionally
• heurter — to offend, to shock (often used for ideas/values)
• blesser — to hurt, to wound (emotionally)
• choquer — to shock, to offend
• vexer — to offend someone's pride (slightly milder)

D5 — Full paragraph re-translation

Now translate the full passage into French from scratch, applying everything you have learned. Aim for fluency, accuracy and A-level register.

"The debate on freedom of expression has been revived in France since the 7 January 2015 attack at the offices of the satirical newspaper Charlie Hebdo, when murderers put an end to the life of cartoonists who had drawn caricatures of the prophet Mohammed. Since then, many have asked whether a society that is becoming more diverse can still accept without difficulty all forms of satire and provocation. Some people think that there are limits to freedom of expression and that, by saying what we want, we can offend and deeply upset certain groups of people. Others think, on the contrary, that it is a fundamental democratic right and that it is essential that the press remain completely free."

Le débat sur la liberté d'expression a été relancé en France depuis l'attentat du 7 janvier 2015 contre la rédaction du journal satirique Charlie Hebdo, quand des tueurs ont mis fin à la vie de dessinateurs qui avaient dessiné des caricatures du prophète Mahomet. Depuis, beaucoup se sont demandé si une société qui devient de plus en plus diversifiée peut encore accepter sans difficulté toutes les formes de satire et de provocation. Certaines personnes pensent que la liberté d'expression a des limites et que, en disant ce que nous voulons, on peut offenser et profondément bouleverser certains groupes de personnes. D'autres pensent au contraire que c'est un droit démocratique fondamental et qu'il est essentiel que la presse reste entièrement libre.

Key structures to check: pluperfect (avaient dessiné), se sont demandé, sans difficulté, c'est un droit, qu'il est essentiel que + subjonctif (reste).
