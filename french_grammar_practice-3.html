Section 2: Infinitive vs Past Participle - 15 DRILLS
⚠️ CRITICAL RULE:
INFINITIVE (to do): ends in -er, -ir, -re → juger, finir, être
Use after: pour, sans, de, à, modal verbs (peut, doit, va)

PAST PARTICIPLE (done): ends in -é, -i, -u → jugé, fini, été
Use after: être, avoir, passive constructions

Test: Can you say "to ___" in English? → Use infinitive
Does it mean "has been ___"? → Use past participle

Section 3: Missing Accents (à, ça, où) - 12 DRILLS
⚠️ CRITICAL RULE:
à (with accent) = to, at, in → Je vais à Paris
a (no accent) = he/she has → Il a un livre

ça (with cedilla) = that → C'est pour ça
sa (no cedilla) = his/her → Sa mère

où (with accent) = where → Où es-tu?
ou (no accent) = or → Café ou thé?

là (with accent) = there → Il est là
la (no accent) = the/her → La maison / Il la voit

Section 4: Agreement (Gender & Number) - 10 DRILLS
⚠️ CRITICAL RULE:
Adjectives must agree with nouns:
• Masculine singular: grand, petit, naturel
• Feminine singular: grande, petite, naturelle (add -e)
• Masculine plural: grands, petits, naturels (add -s)
• Feminine plural: grandes, petites, naturelles (add -es)

Verbs must agree with subjects:
• Singular subject → singular verb: Il fait, Elle est
• Plural subject → plural verb: Ils font, Elles sont
• Multiple subjects = plural: Marie et Jean sont...

Section 5: Reflexive Pronouns (se vs le/la) - 8 DRILLS
⚠️ CRITICAL RULE:
Reflexive (action on oneself): se protéger, se défendre, se rappeler
→ pour SE protéger (to protect himself/oneself)

Direct object (action on someone/something else):
→ pour LE protéger (to protect him)
→ pour LA protéger (to protect her/it-feminine)

Test: Is the action done to oneself? → Use SE

Section 6: Avoiding Repetition - 5 EXTENDED DRILLS
