CDI vs CDD Contracts (Questions 1-5)

A-Level

The French labour market is characterized by the coexistence of CDI and CDD contracts. CDIs provide permanent employment with strong job protection, while CDDs offer temporary solutions for specific business needs. This dual system creates tension between employers seeking flexibility and employees desiring stability.

The 35-Hour Week (Questions 6-10)

A-Level

The 35-hour working week legislation, implemented in 2000, represents one of France's most significant social reforms. While aiming to reduce unemployment through work-sharing, it has generated ongoing debate about its economic impact and administrative complexity.

Wage Negotiations (Questions 11-15)

A-Level

France's wage negotiation system operates across multiple levels, from national branch agreements to company-specific discussions. Recent reforms attempting to decentralize this process have faced resistance from unions concerned about preserving collective bargaining power.
